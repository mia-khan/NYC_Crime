Our Data, Its Origin, Its Significance

Our dataset is sourced directly from the New York Police Department‚Äôs official park crime statistics, covering the last eight quarters (from Q1 2023 through Q4 2024). We combined and cleaned these datasets to create a unified view of crime trends in New York City parks over time.

After merging the quarterly data files, the final dataset contains approximately 9,232 observations, which far exceeds the minimum requirement for this project. These observations represent reported incidents of seven major felony crime types within NYC parks.

Key Features Included

  • Crime Type: Murder, rape, robbery, felony assault, burglary, grand larceny, grand larceny of a motor vehicle
  • Park Details: Name, size, type (category), and borough location
  • Time Frame: Quarterly breakdown from 2023 to 2024
  • Attributes: A balanced mix of categorical and continuous variables

All records were preprocessed to ensure consistency in formatting, remove duplicates, and handle missing values. Categorical fields such as borough and crime type were standardized for clarity in visualizations.

This cleaned and enriched dataset allows us to explore how crime varies across NYC‚Äôs boroughs, types of parks, and time, forming the foundation of our visual analysis and insights.
