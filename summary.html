NYC Park Crime Analysis: Summary and Findings

New York City parks are vital urban sanctuaries where safety is paramount. This analysis examines major felony crimes in NYC parks (2023-2024), revealing how borough location, park characteristics, and seasonal factors influence crime patterns.

Geographic Distribution

  • Manhattan shows the highest crime concentration despite its relatively small area, likely due to high visitor density and tourist activity.
  • Staten Island maintains the lowest crime rates across all categories, suggesting different usage patterns and demographic factors.
  • These disparities highlight the need for location-specific safety strategies rather than uniform citywide approaches.

Crime Type Patterns

  • Grand larceny dominates across all boroughs but is especially prevalent in Manhattan parks.
  • Felony assault remains significant across all five boroughs, requiring focused prevention.
  • Violent crimes like murder and rape occur at much lower rates but require continued vigilance.
  • Each borough's unique crime profile suggests the need for tailored safety interventions.

Park Size and Crime Relationship

  • The correlation between park size and crime rates is not linear. While larger parks tend to have more incidents, many exceptions exist.
  • Several outliers show that some large parks maintain low crime counts while certain smaller parks experience high crime rates.
  • Design factors including visibility, access points, and layout appear more influential than size alone.
  • This finding suggests safety interventions should focus on specific design elements rather than simply park dimensions.

Temporal Trends

  • Seasonal patterns show notable crime increases during Q2 and Q3 (spring/summer months).
  • Summer peaks are particularly evident for grand larceny and robbery, correlating with increased park usage.
  • Year-over-year comparison shows modest safety improvements from 2023 to 2024.
  • Property crimes show more pronounced seasonal fluctuations than violent offenses.

Main Conclusions & Future Work

Main Conclusions

Our analysis challenges conventional assumptions about urban park safety:

  1. Safety is not uniform across NYC parks - Manhattan's high crime rates contrast with Staten Island's lower rates, indicating location context matters more than borough-wide generalizations.
  2. Crime profiles vary significantly by area - Grand larceny dominates Manhattan while the Bronx shows higher rates of violent crimes, requiring tailored approaches.
  3. Park size doesn't predict safety - Other factors like design, visibility, and surrounding neighborhood characteristics play more decisive roles.
  4. Seasonal patterns offer actionable insights - The predictable increase in summer crimes provides clear opportunities for strategic resource allocation.

Future Work and Recommendations

To build upon these findings and create safer parks, we recommend:

  1. Case studies of outlier parks - Investigate locations that defy expected trends to identify transferable safety practices.
  2. Development of a park safety index - Create a metric incorporating crime data with design features and usage patterns.
  3. Targeted design interventions - Test specific improvements in high-crime parks (lighting, sightlines, access controls).
  4. Expanded temporal analysis - Incorporate time-of-day patterns and historical data to identify longer-term trends.
  5. Integration of community perception - Combine statistics with community feedback to better understand perceived versus actual safety.

These approaches can help NYC develop more effective, data-driven park safety strategies while efficiently allocating resources where they'll have the greatest impact.
