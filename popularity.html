While the typical Ph·ªü gets serves in minutes at a restaurant, its prepartion requires patience and the utmost attention to detail. The best bowls require over 24 hours to slowly simmer deliberately chosen herbs and spices. Beef quality and the freshness of the rice noodles are also critical. Ultimately, authentically cooking the Vietnamese dish at home is an incredibly gratifying culinary experience that will reward you with hearty broth, nutritional value, and versatility.

Discover how to make Ph·ªü:
  1. Prepare the stock: using bones to make the stock. Add onion, ginger, cumin, cinnamon, and allspice and simmer slowly for 3-4 hours, seasoning with salt, sugar, and fish sauce to taste.
  2. Progress the meat: If making Beef Pho, thinly slice the raw beef and cook it in the hot broth. If making chicken Pho, cook the chicken breasts or thighs and tear them into small pieces.
  3. Cook the rice noodles: soften the rice noodles in hot water, then cook them quickly, drain, and set aside.
  4. Assemble Pho: Place cooked rice noodles in a bowl, add chopped beef or chicken, then pour in the hot stock.
  5. Add Side Dishes: Common side dishes include fresh bean sprouts, mint, cilantro, basil, lime, green onions, and chili flakes. You can add fish sauce, chili sauce, or sweet sauce to taste for flavor.
