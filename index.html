As part of what is arguably the world's greatest city, parks are essential urban pockets that not only give residents valuable recreational opportunities but also serve as the center of the community where safety must be supreme. The project we chose dives into the heavy-hearted patterns of major felony criminal activity in the city's green spaces, looking at how boroughs, park type, or perhaps even size can affect severity through a diverse series of data visualizations. We believe this is important and timely work. Robbery, grand larceny, and murder are amongst the group of categories we exploreâ€”all categories that can violently derail a person's life and, by extension, the community's well-being. Identifying these critical patterns is the first step in the right direction to make sure that future policy-making and park design fortify safety for all.
